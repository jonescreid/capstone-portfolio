01 — About Me

A builder, a connector, and a Carolina Gamecock.

My name is Carson Jones. I'm a rising junior at the Darla Moore School of Business, double majoring in Finance and Supply Chain Management with a minor in Information Technology. I grew up in Cincinnati, Ohio, and came to Columbia not entirely sure what to expect - just a certainty that I wanted to be around people who were going somewhere and make something real out of four years.

On campus, you'll find me leading tours as an assistant director in the Moore School Ambassador Program, serving as a brother and chaplain in Alpha Kappa Psi Professional Fraternity, running with the Run With Christ Columbia community I helped start, or somewhere in between Student Government, Club Tennis, Sigma Phi Epsilon, and the IDEA Living and Learning Community. It's a lot - but community doesn't happen on its own.

The Capstone Scholars Program put me in rooms I wouldn't have found otherwise. In my freshman Capstone University 101 section, I met a classmate who introduced me to professional fraternities. That conversation led me to Alpha Kappa Psi - one of the most formative decisions of my college career. That same U101 course is also the reason I'm co-teaching a section this fall as a peer leader, working alongside the same instructor I had as a freshman. Capstone didn't just give me resources. It gave me people.

02 — Looking Ahead
"I live on a golden ocean."

Who I've become, and where I'm going.

When I walked into Capstone as a freshman, I didn't have some grand vision. I knew I wanted community and I knew I wanted to grow. What I didn't expect was that the most important thing I'd learn in two years wouldn't be a concept or a framework - it would be the value of slowing down.

College has a way of accelerating everything. There's always a next internship to land, a next application to submit, a next credential to collect. For a while, I ran that race. And I was decent at it. But somewhere along the way, I shifted. I stopped asking "what's next?" quite so frantically and started asking "what am I building here, in Columbia, right now?" That question changed everything.

What I've learned about myself is that I'm a builder. Not just in the entrepreneurial sense - though I've done that too - but in the relational sense. I genuinely love creating the conditions where people can find each other. That's why I started Run With Christ Columbia. That's why I care about the Ambassador Program as much as the tours themselves. That's why teaching U101 this fall feels like one of the most meaningful things I'll do at Carolina.

Over the next two years, I have two north stars. The first is to build community programs, events, and organizations where Carolina students can find genuine connection - not just networking, but actual belonging. The second is to leave behind something that underclassmen can look at as a blueprint: not for how to pad a resume, but for how to be truly fulfilled by four years. A legacy isn't a plaque. It's a feeling people carry with them after you're gone.

💼

Professionally

Pursue a career in wealth management, building on my finance foundation and the networking I've cultivated across Charlotte and beyond.

🤝

Socially

Create and sustain community platforms that give Carolina students real spaces to belong - not just for today, but for the students who come after.

🌱

Personally

Keep learning what it means to lead with generosity, presence, and faith - and pass that on to anyone willing to walk alongside me.
